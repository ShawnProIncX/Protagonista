Surplus Funds & Tax Facts

1
$68 Billion in unclaimed funds are currently held by U.S. state governments — money belonging to real people who simply don't know it's there.
2
After a foreclosure sale, if the property sells for more than the mortgage balance, the former owner is legally entitled to that surplus — but most never claim it.
3
Tax overpayments are extremely common. Millions of businesses and individuals overpay federal and state taxes each year without realizing they can apply for a refund.
4
Unclaimed funds never expire in most U.S. states — money held from a deceased relative's account 20 years ago can still be claimed by rightful heirs today.
5
1 in 10 Americans has unclaimed property waiting for them. The average claim value is over $1,000 — and many are worth tens of thousands of dollars.
